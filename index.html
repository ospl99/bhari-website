My Story

I've been an operator longer than I've been an investor.

In 1986, I co-founded Ontrack Systems — a technology company I built over 27 years, expanding from India to the UK, UAE, Holland, and Australia. I know what it's like to make payroll, find product-market fit, lose a client, and rebuild from the ground up.

Before that, in 1996, I started Calcutta Online — one of India's first internet companies. Time Magazine featured it. It was acquired by a NASDAQ-listed firm. The internet was young, and so was I.

Those years taught me something no MBA ever could: building is hard, and the founders who survive are the ones worth backing. That's what brought me to angel investing — not as a side hustle, but as a calling.

Investment Approach

High conviction. Early entry. Hands on.

I don't spray and pray. I follow a concentrated, operator-first approach — backing founders early, often as the first cheque, and staying committed through the highs and the lows.

My focus areas: QSR, consumer brands, applied AI, D2C, and technology. I look for founders who are relentless, honest, and deeply curious — building on unit economics that make sense before the pitch deck says they will.

I lead structured engagement across my portfolio companies, driving OKRs, strategy, fundraising, and key hiring decisions. When things get hard, I get closer — not farther.

Transparency

What didn't work — and why it matters.

I've had 13 write-offs. I'm transparent about them because I believe it's important. Here's what they taught me:

01

Governance is non-negotiable

Three companies failed because founders weren't straight with their investors. Railofy, Bharat Agri, Clensta — governance erosion destroyed value. I won't make that mistake again.

02

Product-market fit before scale

Pluspin and MaxWholesale raised well but never found PMF. Great pitch decks don't make great businesses. I now watch for this with an operator's eye.

03

Founder focus is everything

Plop Chat Stories lost its way because the founder was chasing too many things. Shopatplaces shut down prematurely. I now watch for this early — focus is a superpower.
